üì• Introduction: What is a PE Investment Memo?

A PE Investment Memo is the core internal document prepared by deal teams to present a potential investment opportunity to the Investment Committee (IC). It outlines:

  • The company and its market
  • The strategic rationale behind the investment
  • Financials, risks, and proposed deal structure
  • Value creation potential and expected returns

Memos are often delivered in slide deck format, especially at Growth Equity or Mid-Market funds, to facilitate quick consumption and discussion.

üîπ Chapter 1.1: Purpose of a PE Investment Memo

Primary Goals:

  • Document the rationale behind a deal
  • Ensure alignment within the investment team and IC
  • Mitigate cognitive bias via structured diligence
  • Support post-deal portfolio tracking

Audiences:

  • Investment Committee (IC)
  • Operating Partners
  • LP co-investors (sometimes)
  • Internal records for post-mortems or audits

üßæ Conclusion

A PE memo is a synthesis of strategy, analytics, storytelling, and stakeholder management. Slide decks are the go-to format for many ICs due to time constraints and collaboration needs.

‚ùì Q&A Section

Q1: Why use slide decks instead of long Word docs?
A: Slide decks are easier to present and digest in meetings, where time is limited. They focus on visuals and summaries, which facilitates executive discussions.

Q2: Are investment memos legally binding?
A: No, but they can be reviewed during post-deal audits or LP diligence to evaluate decision quality.

Q3: How much time is typically spent on preparing a memo?
A: Weeks to months, depending on the depth of diligence and internal processes. IC-quality memos go through several revisions.
