PE Memo Deck Structure – Chapter 2.1

📘 PE Memo – Ideal Slide Deck Flow (Chapter 2.1)

🧠 Overview

This lesson offers a comprehensive breakdown of the ideal structure of a slide-based investment memo used in private equity. It helps you craft clear, compelling, and logically sequenced presentations that drive alignment and action from your investment committee (IC).

📥 Introduction

Investment committees are busy, critical thinkers. Your deck must guide them through:

  • What the company is
  • Why the investment makes sense
  • Where value will be created
  • What the risks are
  • How the returns look

An ideal slide deck is more than a collection of charts—it is a narrative document designed to move stakeholders from curiosity to conviction.

📚 Chapters

🔹 1. Cover Slide

Purpose: Introduce the investment

  • Company name + logo
  • Target industry
  • Presentation date
  • Team members
  • Internal project codename (if used)

🔹 2. Table of Contents / Deal Summary

Purpose: Preview the deck and give fast access to key sections

Options:

  • Classic table of contents
  • One-pager deal summary

🔹 3. Executive Summary

Purpose: Provide a 1-slide snapshot of the entire memo

  • Company background and size
  • Reason for sale / deal dynamics
  • Investment highlights
  • Headline financials
  • Proposed structure
  • Target returns

🔹 4. Business Overview

Purpose: Explain what the company does and how it makes money

  • Products/services
  • Customers and segments
  • Revenue mix
  • Locations/geographies
  • Org chart or leadership
  • Recent historical performance

🔹 5. Market Overview

Purpose: Set the context for market potential

  • TAM, SAM
  • Growth trends (CAGR)
  • Industry dynamics
  • Customer behavior
  • Competitive analysis

🔹 6. Investment Thesis

Purpose: Justify the rationale to invest

  • 3–5 core thesis points
  • Tailwinds, wedge, fit to fund strategy
  • Clear KPIs or visual support

🔹 7. Due Diligence Highlights

Purpose: Validate thesis and uncover red/yellow flags

  • Commercial and operational DD
  • Legal, accounting, technical
  • QoE insights and adjustments

🔹 8. Financials

Purpose: Present historicals and forecasts

  • 3–5 year financials
  • Base / Upside / Downside cases
  • Key performance indicators (LTV, CAC)
  • Waterfall/bridge visuals

🔹 9. Value Creation Plan (VCP)

Purpose: Show how PE will create value

  • Growth and margin strategies
  • Operational playbooks and 100-day plans
  • Visual timelines and OKRs

🔹 10. Risks & Mitigants

Purpose: Flag risks and defend against them

Risk Description Mitigation
Market Saturation Growth may slow in mature geos Expand into LATAM or product line extension
Execution Risk New GTM strategy might fail Hire CRO with proven track record

🔹 11. Returns & Exit Strategy

Purpose: Show the payoff

  • IRR and MoIC calculations
  • Entry/exit multiples
  • Exit scenarios and comps
  • Sensitivity tables

🔹 12. Appendix

Purpose: Provide reference depth

  • Full financial models
  • Market studies
  • Customer interviews
  • Org charts and resumes

✅ Quick Checklist

  • Is each slide answering a key diligence question?
  • Are you combining data, visuals, and narrative?
  • Do you avoid filler slides and focus only on drivers?
  • Does the structure match the flow of logic?
  • Have you tested the story on a peer?
