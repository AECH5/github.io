PE Memo Slide Breakdown – Chapter 2.2

📘 PE Memo – Slide-by-Slide Breakdown (Chapter 2.2)

🧠 Overview

This chapter provides a detailed breakdown of each slide commonly included in a PE investment memo deck. Learn how to structure your content, design with clarity, and anticipate your audience’s questions and expectations.

📥 Introduction

Every slide must deliver one self-contained insight. Build your deck so it can be skimmed in under 10 minutes, with each section answering a key investment question.

📚 Slide-by-Slide Guide

1️⃣ Cover Slide

  • Target company name and logo
  • Investment type (buyout, growth equity, etc.)
  • Team members and date
  • IC round (e.g., “IC2 – Final Approval”)

2️⃣ Table of Contents / Deal Summary

Options:

  • Classic TOC slide
  • 1-slide deal summary: ARR, NRR, TAM, thesis bullets

3️⃣ Executive Summary

  • Company description
  • 2–3 thesis bullets
  • Headline financials (Revenue, EBITDA, CAGR)
  • Capital structure overview
  • IRR / MoIC targets

4️⃣ Business Overview

  • Business model and product/services
  • Revenue mix
  • Customer segments
  • Go-to-market strategy
  • Leadership team/org structure

5️⃣ Market Overview

  • Market size (TAM/SAM)
  • Growth rates (CAGR)
  • Fragmentation / consolidation dynamics
  • Competitor landscape (map or matrix)

6️⃣ Investment Thesis

  • 3–5 core thesis points (specific, not generic)
  • Use supporting visuals for each thesis
  • Tailor to fund strategy (platforms, buy-and-build, etc.)

7️⃣ Due Diligence Summary

8️⃣ Financials

  • Historical P&L summary
  • Base / Upside / Downside forecast scenarios
  • Key metrics: LTV, CAC, churn, NRR
  • Bridge charts and visual deltas

9️⃣ Value Creation Plan (VCP)

  • Revenue drivers (GTM, pricing, expansion)
  • Margin expansion (costs, automation)
  • Roadmap: 100-day plan and milestones
  • Before/after visuals for margin or EBITDA impact

🧰 Slide Creation Principles Recap

  • One key idea per slide
  • Bold takeaway headlines
  • Replace walls of text with visuals
  • Assume the audience is skimming
