PE Memo – Slide 1: Cover Slide

📘 Deep Dive: Slide 1 – Cover Slide of a PE Investment Memo Deck

🧠 Purpose of the Cover Slide

The Cover Slide serves as the front door to your investment memo deck. It sets the tone for what’s to come and provides basic orientation to your audience (typically the Investment Committee, senior partners, or advisors).

This slide must be:

  • Clean and professional
  • Minimalist in content (no data)
  • Branded with the target company identity
  • Clearly date-stamped and owner-identified

Think of it like the cover of a deal prospectus—clear, elegant, and aligned with the tone of your firm's brand.

🎯 Strategic Role in the Deck

Although not analytical, the cover slide plays several important roles:

  • Orientation – Ensures the IC knows which deal this is.
  • Branding – Helps reinforce the professionalism and visual standards of your firm.
  • Version Control – Prevents confusion between IC1, IC2, and final presentations.
  • Signaling – A sloppy or cluttered cover raises flags before you say a word.
Tip: A crisp, clean cover slide signals that the rest of the deck will be clear, focused, and well-structured.

💡 Best Practices

  • ✅ Use high-resolution logos (for both target and your firm)
  • ✅ Ensure alignment and symmetry — no misaligned logos or text
  • ✅ Avoid clutter or long descriptions
  • ✅ Save version-controlled file names (e.g., Orion_IC1_vFinal_May2025.pptx)
  • ✅ Use your firm's PowerPoint master slide if available

❌ Common Mistakes

  • ❌ Overloading the cover with bullet points or thesis blurbs
  • ❌ Using low-quality logos or mismatched branding
  • ❌ Skipping the date or version (confusing in multi-round ICs)
  • ❌ Leaving in placeholders (e.g., “Insert Logo Here”)

✅ Quick Checklist

  • Is the target company’s name and logo clearly visible?
  • Is the presentation clearly dated?
  • Are the presenters or deal team listed (if relevant)?
  • Is the slide visually clean and brand-consistent?
  • Does it convey professionalism and polish?
